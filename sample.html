#117:小孩喜欢看电视的原因和解决方法

Studies suggest that children spend more time watching TV than they did in the past and spend less on doing active or creative things. Why do you think it is the case? What measures and methods can be used to tackle it?
开头段替换词

doing active or creative things: lead an active life, engage in outdoor and creative activities. 


开头段范例

Research has shown that children today spend many hours watching TV, instead of doing outdoor or creative activities. This is a problem which merits attention and demands solutions. (7)

There is ample evidence that children's screen time has increased rapidly and displaced the time for outdoor or creative activities. Parents should take immediate action to fix this problem. (7)

范文:

The first reason why children spend more time watching TV than ever is that their parents lead a busy life and have no time to accompany children to do different activities. Adults have to work long hours, attend networking events, travel for business and receive training, which can affect their family life. Therefore, they permit children to watch TV, which can easily grab attention, and  children would not interfere with their work. In contrast, parents are unwilling to allow children to do creative or active activities outside home, which may not be safe.  (7+)


The first reason why children today are addicted to television viewing is that they cannot do other extra-curricula activities without the company of their parents. Struggling to survive a competitive world of work, adults have to work long hours every day, and their leisure time is predominantly used for other work-related activities, such as attending networking events, business trips and job training. They would rather see their children watch TV safely at home, than do other activities, such as playing sport or games with others, since they are afraid that their children are susceptible to influences outside home. (7+)

范文:

Besides, there is less open space available for playing sport and doing creative activities. Playgrounds and parks have been closed to make way for housing development and the construction of new roads, so children cannot find any place to play games. They are also not able to find commuty centres or other places where they can engage in art activities. (6.5)

Another cause of increased screen time among today’s children is the limited access to open spaces and facilities suitable for other activities. They cannot find any place in the community to play games and sport with their peers since playgrounds and parks have been closed to make way for housing development and the construction of new roads. Nor can they find community centres or other places where they can engage in art activities, as most land in cities has been saved for the construction of residential or business buildings. (7+)

范文:

There are two solutions to this problem. Firstly, schools can increase facilities and run courses to allow children to play sport and attend art courses after school. They can thus find enjoyment in these activities. Secondly, the government can launch awareness campaigns to encourage parents to spend more time with their children. Adults should understand the importance of forming some positive habits in childhood. The involvement of parents can help shift children’s attention from TV programmes to other activities. (6.5)

This problem can be solved possibly by two methods. The first approach is to increase facilities and run courses at school, offering children more options after they finish school work. They can find enjoyment in playing sport, learning painting and playing with legos before their parents pick them up. In addition to this, the government can launch awareness campaigns to encourage parents to spend more time with their children, by highlighting the fact that forming some positive habits such as playing a music instrument can benefit children for a lifetime. Parents who realise the chronic effect of television viewing will take a more active role in children’s leisure activities. (7+)


结尾段范例

In conclusion, parents and schools should play a bigger part in changing the ways children spend their time after school.
